## RATIONALE OF THE STUDY
Capstone projects are integral to achieving a higher level of education because they are a culmination of learning activities throughout the entire curriculum. Students bring forth skills they have learned in the classroom and have to solve real-world challenges through interdisciplinary research. A lot of academic institutions set up their capstone projects to be done in groups, necessitating the coordination, collaboration, and the mastering of project management elements needed to successfully finish the project. As with any project, the capstone involves students, faculty advisers, panelists, and program coordinators, and each has their own part to play. Every participant fulfills a particular role, managing, guiding, evaluating, and overseeing every stage of the project lifecycle, from proposal to final defense. However, it appears today the management of these projects relies more on individual systems, manual tracking, and poor integration of comms systems which ultimately mismanagement, misinformation, and challenges in tracking development of the entire project. The way capstone projects are achieved starts with document sharing, communicating, and tracking progress using different platforms, and then piecing them together into what seems to be an organized document. Much of the time, the version used is different, and there is much different completion with unclear communication to all parties responsible. The absence of a system is not easy for faculty and students to understand, with faculty not being able to track many projects at once, and students not understanding the bare minimum of their progress and deadlines. These issues clearly show the need for an organized system to enhance communication among all parties involved, in a system that is web based. With all parties able to see the progress of all other parties, the management of the system keeps external communication efficiency in focus. one supposed to improve the overall experience of the students and faculty members.

The scholarly community has dedicated a lot of efforts in studying the incorporation of capstone projects in education. These projects obtain and narrow together a multitude of skills providing an easy assimilation into the working/advanced study field. They become an asset in professional workplace settings. This integrating knowledge and skills from various competencies and practical fields is beneficial also in engineering, computer science, business, and health science [1]. In particular, for capstone projects, there are multiple challenges. Research shows the weaker the technique, the stronger the inefficacies, thus project delays, lack of coordinated efforts, and poor intermingle, as well as poor project quality. The classic technique of capstone projects, in which a plethora of unassociated resources are collected like the use of a spreadsheet, email, and various communications tools tend to be the most ineffective, and thus the most prone to hoax [3]. The overall essence in the functionality of the network is poor administrative efforts that lead to weaker functioning of the network in managing communication, progress, and shared files. This poor communication leads to a heavier workload on more stress for students and more frustration and little support for faculty staff in administration [4]. Addressing these challenges, there has been growing interest in technology as a means to facilitate project management in educational settings. Web-based project management systems have been demonstrated to enhance coordination, communication, and efficiency in a variety of settings [5]. In an academic setting, tools like Trello, Asana, or Microsoft Project have been adopted where task assignment, status updates, and document management and sharing are features common to their use [6]. These tools, however, are not tailored for use with capstone projects and are unlikely to have the requisite functionality to address the more sophisticated needs of academic capstone experiences [7]. More recent works have underscored the absence of adequate systems for capstone project management. Such systems need to provide features like milestone management, role-based access control, document control systems, scheduling for defenses and presentations, and integrated communications [8]. These systems are meant to compliment, rather than burden, the users, namely, students, faculty, and administrators [9].

## REVIEW OF RELATED WORKS
Different systems and tools have been created to help manage projects in schools and universities. These tools are useful, but most of them are not made for capstone projects, which have unique needs compared to regular academic tasks.

One common tool is Trello, a web-based project manager that uses boards, lists, and cards to organize tasks. It is simple and easy to use, but it is limited for academic use. For example, it does not have features like milestone tracking based on school timelines, role-based access for students and faculty, or scheduling that matches academic activities [11].

Another popular tool is Asana, which lets users assign tasks, set deadlines, and work with teammates. It is good for teamwork and tracking progress, but just like Trello, it is a general tool. It does not include functions such as version control for documents, scheduling of defenses, or built-in communication features for faculty and students [12].

Microsoft Project is more advanced and has tools for scheduling and resource management. However, it is complicated and needs training before it can be used well. Also, it is not really made for schools, so it may be difficult to use for capstone projects that involve both students and faculty with different skill levels [13].

Some schools have created their own custom systems by adding capstone modules to their learning management systems (LMS). These often have basic features like submission of files, grading, and communication. But they usually lack strong tools for project tracking, milestone planning, and real-time collaboration [14].

More recently, systems designed only for capstone projects have appeared. One example is Capstrack, a web-based platform that supports engineering and computer science programs. It provides team formation, project proposal submission, milestone tracking, and faculty evaluation. While it is a step forward, it still does not have more advanced tools like full document management with version control, defense scheduling, or activity tracking [15].

## PROJECT OBJECTIVES
The CapTrack project is guided by a set of objectives that aim to address the challenges often experienced in capstone project management. It seeks to provide an effective and practical solution that will benefit students, advisers, panel members, and coordinators. By offering tools that simplify management, improve collaboration, and promote accountability, CapTrack ensures that every stage of the project is more organized and transparent.

**General Goals:**
*   **Streamline Capstone Project Management** – Build a centralized platform that makes handling capstone projects easier and more efficient throughout the entire process.
*   **Enhance Collaboration and Communication** – Support stronger interaction and teamwork between students, faculty advisers, panelists, and program coordinators.
*   **Improve Transparency and Accountability** – Provide clear tools for tracking progress, monitoring individual contributions, and ensuring responsibility in all stages of the project.

**Specific Goals:**

*Functional Goals*
1.  **Milestone Tracking System:** Implement a visual system for tracking project progress through various stages (Proposal, Capstone 1, Capstone 2, Final Defense) using progress bars, Kanban boards, and milestone checklists.
2.  **Task Scheduler:** Develop a feature that allows for the creation, assignment, and tracking of tasks with deadlines and progress updates.
3.  **Team Management Module:** Create a module for assigning responsibilities, tracking member contributions, and mapping faculty roles (Coordinator, Adviser, Panel, Chair).
4.  **Document Upload and Feedback System:** Implement a system for uploading documents with basic file management, feedback capabilities, and threaded commenting for collaborative review and improvement.
5.  **Notification System:** Design an in-app notification system that alerts users to deadlines, approvals, feedback, and schedule updates.
6.  **Defense Schedule Manager:** Develop a feature for managing defense schedules, including slot requests, approvals, and panel assignments.
7.  **Project Timeline View:** Create a visual representation of project deliverables and deadlines in calendar or Gantt format.
8.  **Activity Logs:** Implement a system for tracking user actions such as file uploads, comments, and task completions for accountability purposes.
9.  **Enhanced 60% Defense Preparation System:** Develop a comprehensive system that includes readiness tracking, requirements checklist, timeline management, and countdown timers to help students prepare effectively for their 60% defense presentations.
10. **Role-Based Access Control:** Establish differentiated access and functionalities based on user roles (SuperAdmin/Chairperson, Coordinator, Faculty Member, Student).

*Non-Functional Goals*
1.  **Usability:** Ensure the system is intuitive and easy to use for all stakeholders, regardless of technical expertise.
2.  **Reliability:** Guarantee that the system is consistently available and performs well under expected usage conditions.
3.  **Security:** Implement robust security measures to protect sensitive data and ensure that users can only access information relevant to their roles.
4.  **Scalability:** Design the system to handle increasing numbers of users and projects without degradation in performance.

## SCOPE AND LIMITATIONS OF THE STUDY
The scope of this study centers on the creation, design, and use of CapTrack, a web-based system for monitoring and managing project milestones and team progress in capstone projects. The system is intended to cover the full life cycle of capstone projects, starting from the proposal stage up to the final defense. It aims to serve all stakeholders, including students, faculty advisers, panel members, and program coordinators.

To solve capstone project management problems like lack of milestone management, lack of access control on project documents, lack of custom feedback, lack of scheduling for defenses, poor structured project timeline, unorganized project logs, limited project team tracking, and others, CapTrack provides capabilities for effective logging, uploading and sharing documents, selective access control on various levels, and tracking of project timelines. Its user friendly and secure design offers peace of mind and easy customization as project and academic requirements evolve.

But this work has several weaknesses that should be noted. To begin with, the system still lacks a real-time chat function. Instead, it relies on threaded comments and notifications as its primary means of interaction. This particular means of interaction operates sufficiently for most communication needs, but it does not fulfill the demands of users who work in a real-time collaborative environment.

The system’s real further web design also means it does not feature a separate mobile application. While it can be used via mobile web browsers, it is likely the overall experience on mobile devices such as smartphones, is not as user friendly as that which can be enjoyed on desktop or laptop computers.

While there are several features viewed as valuable which are not included in the system’s main application, the provision of SMS notifications is an example. Even the first version is unable to support basic integrations with external calendars like Google Calendar or iCal, which is a big limitation.

Another limitation, the system does not include detailed analytics dashboards that show progress metrics. These are seen as extra features, not essential for the main functionality of the system. In the same way, the initial implementation does not include in-platform PDF preview capabilities or advanced version control features.

Finally, the study looks at the development and initial implementation of the system but does not provide a thorough evaluation of its long-term effects on capstone project outcomes. Further research is needed to determine how well the system improves project quality, student learning, and faculty efficiency over time.
