# CapTracks System Flow (Start to Finish)

This guide explains the full CapTracks process in order, from system setup to final defense results.

## 1) System Access and Authentication

1. User opens the system and logs in using School ID and password.
2. The system checks whether the account is:
   - Faculty (`UserAccount`) or
   - Student (`StudentAccount`)
3. After successful login, the user is redirected to the correct dashboard based on role:
   - Chairperson
   - Coordinator
   - Adviser / Panelist / Teacher
   - Student
4. For students with first-time access, password update is required before proceeding.

## 2) Chairperson Phase (Academic Setup)

The chairperson prepares the environment for the term:

1. Manages academic terms (activate/archive term).
2. Creates and manages class offerings.
3. Imports or manages faculty records.
4. Imports or manages student records.
5. Enrolls students into offerings.
6. Assigns coordinator roles to faculty when needed.

Output of this phase: students and faculty are ready and organized in the active term.

## 3) Student Phase (Group Formation and Project Start)

After chairperson setup, students begin the core project flow:

1. Access dashboard and view assigned context (group, tasks, submissions).
2. Create group (or accept invitation to join one).
3. Invite members (max group size constraints are enforced).
4. Invite an adviser; adviser accepts or declines.
5. Submit project proposal versions.
6. Receive comments/review feedback from coordinator/adviser.
7. Manage milestones and task assignments in the group.
8. Upload task submissions/evidence and update progress.
9. Continue iterations until milestone targets are met.

Output of this phase: a complete group is formed, adviser invitation is sent/accepted, and project work is active.

## 4) Coordinator Phase (Operational Monitoring and Controls)

The coordinator supports and controls execution after student groups begin:

1. Reviews class list and imported students.
2. Monitors faculty matrix/workload.
3. Oversees existing groups and updates group records when needed.
4. Creates milestone templates and tasks.
5. Assigns milestones to groups.
6. Reviews proposal submissions and feedback cycles.
7. Monitors group progress and readiness for defense.
8. Maintains defense rubrics and defense schedule records.

Output of this phase: groups are monitored and prepared for formal defense processing.

## 5) Adviser / Panel Phase (Mentoring and Evaluation)

Advisers and panelists interact with assigned groups:

1. Respond to adviser invitations.
2. Review group details, milestones, and submissions.
3. Provide feedback and comments on proposals/tasks.
4. Respond to panel invitations for defense schedules.
5. During defense windows, complete rating sheets for assigned schedules.

Output of this phase: guided groups with documented evaluations.

## 6) Defense Request and Scheduling Flow

When a group is ready:

1. Student group submits defense request (`proposal`, `60_percent`, or `100_percent`).
2. System validates eligibility:
   - Group exists
   - Adviser is assigned
   - Required progress threshold is met for 60%/100%
   - No duplicate pending request of same type
3. Coordinator reviews request:
   - Approve or reject
   - If approved, creates/updates defense schedule and panel assignment
4. Notifications are sent to relevant roles.

Output of this phase: an approved, scheduled defense with assigned evaluators.

## 7) Defense Day and Rating Consolidation

1. Adviser/panel members submit individual ratings via rating sheets.
2. Coordinator views aggregated ratings.
3. Coordinator can print rating sheets/results.
4. Coordinator finalizes ratings (or reopens if corrections are needed).

Output of this phase: finalized defense evaluation for the scheduled defense.

## 8) End-to-End Lifecycle Summary

1. **Chairperson** sets terms, offerings, students, and faculty.
2. **Coordinator** sets milestones, manages groups, and prepares defense structures.
3. **Students** form groups, work milestones, and submit proposals/tasks.
4. **Adviser/Panel** reviews work and rates defenses.
5. **Coordinator** schedules, consolidates, and finalizes defense outcomes.

This is the full CapTracks flow from initial setup to final defense completion.

## Full Step-by-Step Flow (Step + Next Format)

**Step 1:** User opens CapTracks and logs in using School ID and password.  
**Next:** Step 2

**Step 2:** System validates if the account is faculty (`UserAccount`) or student (`StudentAccount`).  
**Next:** Step 3

**Step 3:** System redirects user to the correct dashboard based on role (Chairperson, Coordinator, Adviser/Panelist/Teacher, Student).  
**Next:** Step 4

**Step 4:** If student is first-time login, system requires password change before allowing normal access.  
**Next:** Step 5

**Step 5:** Chairperson activates the academic term to open the cycle for the semester.  
**Next:** Step 6

**Step 6:** Chairperson creates and manages class offerings for the active term.  
**Next:** Step 7

**Step 7:** Chairperson imports/manages faculty records and student records.  
**Next:** Step 8

**Step 8:** Chairperson enrolls students into offerings and assigns coordinator role(s) where needed.  
**Next:** Step 9

**Step 9:** Students log in and access dashboard context (group, tasks, milestones, submissions).  
**Next:** Step 10

**Step 10:** Student creates a group or accepts a pending group invitation.  
**Next:** Step 11

**Step 11:** Group leader invites members (system enforces group size and eligibility constraints).  
**Next:** Step 12

**Step 12:** Group leader invites adviser for the group.  
**Next:** Step 13

**Step 13:** Adviser accepts (or declines) invitation; if accepted, adviser becomes active mentor for the group.  
**Next:** Step 14

**Step 14:** Students submit project proposal versions.  
**Next:** Step 15

**Step 15:** Adviser/Coordinator review proposals and provide comments/feedback; students iterate revisions.  
**Next:** Step 16

**Step 16:** Coordinator manages milestone templates and assigns milestones/tasks to groups.  
**Next:** Step 17

**Step 17:** Students execute milestones, assign tasks internally, and upload task/project submissions as evidence.  
**Next:** Step 18

**Step 18:** Coordinator monitors group progress, proposal status, and overall readiness for defense.  
**Next:** Step 19

**Step 19:** When requirements are met, group submits a defense request (`proposal`, `60_percent`, or `100_percent`).  
**Next:** Step 20

**Step 20:** System validates defense request eligibility (group exists, adviser assigned, progress threshold met, no duplicate pending request).  
**Next:** Step 21

**Step 21:** Coordinator reviews request and decides to approve or reject.  
**Next:** Step 22

**Step 22:** If approved, coordinator creates/updates defense schedule and panel assignments, then notifies stakeholders.  
**Next:** Step 23

**Step 23:** During defense, adviser/panel members evaluate and submit rating sheets.  
**Next:** Step 24

**Step 24:** Coordinator reviews consolidated ratings, prints outputs if needed, and finalizes results (or reopens if correction is required).  
**Next:** Step 25

**Step 25:** Final defense outcome is recorded and released.  
**Next:** End of process
